Sparring
  • Student should be able to adequately execute jabs, crosses, hooks, and reverse punches while sparring
  • Student should be able to demonstrate effective usage of forward fighting stance
  • Student should be able to advance and retreat from combat without turning his/her back
  • Student should have an understanding of 'strong-side' techniques vs. 'weak-side' techniques
  • Student should be able to demonstrate basic use of the guard, as well as upper-body blocks and jamming techniques
  • Student should be able to adequately execute inverted hooks, uppercuts, chops, and forearm strikes
  • Student should be able to defend hisself/herself against successive combinations
  • Student should make extensive use of both hands and feet as weapons
  • Student should demonstrate development of an arsenal of combinations
  • Student should begin to develop usage of bobbing and weaving to avoid strikes
  • Student should begin to demonstrate effective use of different stances to generate power and absorb attacks
  • Student should begin to avoid fighting in a straight line, instead employing circling his/her opponent
  • Student should begin to identify and exercise control over his/her telegraphs
  • Student should avoid throwing a single strike or kick and instead throws multiple attacks in succession
  • Student should begin to utilize strategy, such as feinting and positioning relative to an opponent's attacks
  • Student should demonstrate effective use of hammer blows, outside crescent kicks, and side kicks while sparring
  • Student should begin to exercise trapping and jamming techniques
  • Student should be comfortable switching stances while sparring and fluidly transition between stances to minimize opportunity for opponent
  • Student should be able to apply simple punch and kick grabs while kickboxing
  • Student should rely on a vast arsenal of attacks, almost never throwing a single technique outside of a combination
  • Student should be proficient in handling situations where their kick is caught by their opponent
  • Student should begin to be introduced into the animal sparring and employ basic techniques learned from form
  • Student should begin to display the ability to follow limbs from opponents' attack back to the head
  • Student should be effective in kickboxing against a variety of opponent sizes and be able to significantly exercise the fighting skills of all low ranks while sparring
  • Student should demonstrate an aptitude for listening and responding to criticsm and critique, exercising discipline during a fight
  • Student should be able control his/her emotions while in the ring
  • Student should begin utilizing the false leg stances to hide telegraphing of kicks and to deceive opponent of weight distribution
  • Student should begin to demonstrate differences in animal styles while animal sparring
  • Student should begin to cultivate a personal animal style which they employ more frequently during animal sparring
  • Student should begin to mentor less experienced students, leaving openings where appropriate to assist partners in developing in the art
  • Student should be able to fight at a variety of different intensity levels, ranging from their current rank to white belt
  • Student should begin developing in more challenging fighting scenarios, such as multi-opponent sparring and blindfolded sparring
  • Student should begin to exercise throws and takedowns during sparring, utilizing appropriate stances and stance-entry techniques
  • Student should be able to deliver kicks and punches with significant power to disable opponent rapidly
  • Student should begin to develop strategies for multi-opponent sparring and should demonstrate effective employment of positioning in these scenarios
  • Student should be able to move silently while working with other students who are blindfolded in the sparring ring
  • Student should be able to effectively strike small targets on opponent's body, utilizing penetrating techniques
  • Student should be able to demonstrate they are able to control opponents' movement through the use of strikes, blocks, feints, and strategy for opponents who are green belt and below
  • Student should demonstrate effective use of snake, cat, crossover, crane, and bow/arrow stances while animal sparring
  • Student should be able to hide his/her telegraphs enough that lower ranks have a significant difficulty in reading the students movements
  • Student should develop conditioning and stamina such that he/she is able to last longer than five minutes while sparring without becoming exhausted
  • Student should be able to overwhelm opponents with a flurry of strikes
  • Student should be able to control themselves such that they do not engage in brawling, even during difficult fights
  • Student should demonstrate control over opponent in all sparring situations
  • Student should demonstrate significant differences between Tiger, Crane, Leopard, Snake, and Dragon kung fu styles while animal sparring
  • Student should be able to execute signature techniques from all five animal styles during animal sparring
  • Student should be able to effectively subdue an opponent through the use of locks, holds, or throws while sparring
  • Student should be able to defend hisself/herself against at least two attackers for two or more minutes
  • Student should be able to analyze other individuals' sparring, either in person or via video, and give effective strategies to students
  • Student should be able to give guidance, when requested, and identify flaws in others' sparring ability to assist them in improving their skills
  • Student should be able to effectively spar at any previous level, in order to give every student a challenge, without overwhelming the student with a situation where they cannot possibly compete
  • Student should be able to guide multiple opponent sparring situations so they don't devolve from the exercise
  • Student should be able to control matches through the use of 'command tone' in order to get students to comply with school rules and prevent injury
